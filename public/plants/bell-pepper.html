Plant care

Light:

Full sun: 6-8+ hours, with fruiting crops performing best with the longest bright exposure.

Soil mix:

Use a rich, well-drained vegetable container mix with compost and a slow-release or organic nutrient source.

Soil moisture:

Evenly moist, never waterlogged.

When to water:

Water deeply whenever the top inch dries. large fruiting crops in containers may need daily water in heat.

Fertilizer:

Feed regularly during active growth and fruiting. avoid excess nitrogen once plants should be flowering and fruiting.

Pruning:

Remove diseased leaves, improve airflow, and prune by crop type; tomatoes may need suckering/staking while peppers need lighter shaping.

Repotting/rootbound:

Start in a container deep enough for the crop. many fruiting vegetables need roughly 5-gallon or larger containers.

Best use

Patio pots and raised beds

Zones: usda zones 4-11

Problems to watch

Aphids, whiteflies, spider mites, flea beetles, caterpillars, bacterial/fungal leaf spots, blossom-end rot, and powdery mildew.

Placement notes

Container-friendly edible crop that can be grown outdoors or indoors with strong light/grow lights.
