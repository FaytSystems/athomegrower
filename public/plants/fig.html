Plant care

Light:

At least 8 hours of sun is best for fruiting.

Soil mix:

Well-drained soil; avoid sites with root-knot nematode problems when possible.

Soil moisture:

Consistent soil moisture improves yield, especially in heat.

When to water:

Aim for deep, regular watering during fruiting; container figs dry quickly in summer.

Fertilizer:

Use light to moderate fertility. too much nitrogen can produce leaves instead of fruit.

Pruning:

Train as a bush form in many home gardens. remove dead wood and head back in spring to control size.

Repotting/rootbound:

For patio trees, use large containers, refresh soil, root-prune when necessary, and avoid letting the root ball dry out.

Best use

Large containers and sunny patios

Zones: usda zones 7-10

Problems to watch

Root-knot nematode, fig rust, fruit souring, pink blight, thread blight, birds, and ants can be issues.
