English Ivy

Hedera helix

A classic trailing ivy with lobed leaves. it is attractive in hanging planters, but it is not safe for pet-accessible spaces.

Plant care

Light:

Follow the plant's light tag, usually bright indirect to medium indoor light.

Soil mix:

Use a well-draining indoor mix matched to the root type; add perlite or bark for airflow.

Soil moisture:

Avoid soggy soil. most indoor toxic ornamentals prefer a dry-down cycle.

When to water:

Water when the top 1-2 inches dry unless the plant page says it needs steadier moisture.

Fertilizer:

Feed lightly in active growth and reduce in winter.

Pruning:

Remove damaged leaves and wipe sap carefully if the plant is irritating. wash hands after pruning.

Repotting/rootbound:

Repot when rootbound or unstable; keep plant waste away from pets and children.

Best use

Decorative plants that should be kept away from pets and children unless verified safe.

Problems to watch

Mealybugs, scale, spider mites, thrips, fungus gnats, and root rot.
