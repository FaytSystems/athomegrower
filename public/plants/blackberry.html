Rubus fruticosus

Plant care

Light:

Full sun is best for strong fruiting.

Soil mix:

Use deep, well-drained, organic-rich soil. acid-loving berries need acidic soil amendments.

Soil moisture:

Consistent moisture, especially during flowering, fruit set, and harvest.

When to water:

Water deeply during dry spells. mulch to keep roots cool and evenly moist.

Fertilizer:

Fertilize in spring based on crop needs and soil testing. avoid excess nitrogen that delays fruiting.

Pruning:

Prune by fruiting habit: remove old canes, dead wood, and crowded stems to improve light and airflow.

Repotting/rootbound:

For containers, refresh mix and root-prune or up-pot when growth declines or roots fill the pot.

Best use

Large containers and edible hedges

Zones: usda zones 5-9

Birds, fruit rots, cane borers, mites, aphids, leaf spots, and rust diseases.
