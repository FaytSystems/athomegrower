Plant care

Light:

Give the plant the light listed in the quick facts and adjust gradually if leaves fade, stretch, or scorch.

Soil mix:

Well-drained soil with moderate fertility and good seed-to-soil contact

Soil moisture:

Avoid leaving roots in stale, soggy soil unless the plant is specifically moisture-loving.

When to water:

Keep evenly moist while establishing, then maintain steady growth moisture

Fertilizer:

Feed lightly during active growth and reduce or pause during slow winter growth.

Pruning:

Remove dead, damaged, or crowded growth with clean pruners.

Repotting/rootbound:

Repot when roots circle densely, the plant dries too fast, or growth stalls despite good care.

Best use

Large-seeded winter legume for nitrogen and sturdy spring biomass

Zones: USDA Zones 6-10

Season: cool-season

Planting window: Sow in early spring or from late summer into fall depending on your region

Termination: Chop, mow, or tarp at early bloom for peak nitrogen return and easier breakdown

Nitrogen: Nitrogen fixer; contributes plant-available nitrogen when terminated at the right stage

Uses: Large-seeded winter legume for nitrogen and sturdy spring biomass. Use as mulch, compost input, soil biology support, erosion control, or rotation support depending on the crop and the timing.

Zone timing

Use these windows as a starting point and adjust for local frost, heat, and rainfall.

Zones 2-4

Sow

April to May in spring, or August to early September in fall

Manage

Use for short spring biomass or quick fall cover before deep cold

Terminate

June in spring cycles, or allow winterkill / chop in fall depending on species

Zones 5-6

Sow

March to April in spring, or August to October in fall

Manage

Use for overwinter cover or shoulder-season bed recovery

Terminate

May to June for overwintered stands, or let winterkill species break down naturally

Zones 7-8

Sow

February to March in spring, or September to November in fall

Manage

Grow through winter in many areas or use in cool shoulder seasons

Terminate

April to May for overwintered stands or as needed before summer crops

Zones 9-11

Sow

October to January in fall/winter, or very early spring if heat arrives late

Manage

Use in the cool season when summer crops are out of the beds

Terminate

March to April before hot weather or before warm-season planting

Problems to watch

Watch for aphids, mites, scale, mealybugs, fungus gnats, leaf spot, and root rot.
