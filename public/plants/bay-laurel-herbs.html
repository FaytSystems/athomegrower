Plant care

Light:

Give the plant the light listed in the quick facts and adjust gradually if leaves fade, stretch, or scorch.

Soil mix:

Well-draining potting mix

Soil moisture:

Avoid leaving roots in stale, soggy soil unless the plant is specifically moisture-loving.

When to water:

Let top soil dry slightly

Fertilizer:

Feed lightly during active growth and reduce or pause during slow winter growth.

Pruning:

Remove dead, damaged, or crowded growth with clean pruners.

Repotting/rootbound:

Repot when roots circle densely, the plant dries too fast, or growth stalls despite good care.

Best use

Culinary, tea, fragrance, and pollinator herbs for containers, shelves, and garden beds.

Problems to watch

Watch for aphids, mites, scale, mealybugs, fungus gnats, leaf spot, and root rot.
