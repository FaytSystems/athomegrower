Plant care

Light:

Give the plant the light listed in the quick facts and adjust gradually if leaves fade, stretch, or scorch.

Soil mix:

Average to fertile, well-drained soil depending on the species

Soil moisture:

Avoid leaving roots in stale, soggy soil unless the plant is specifically moisture-loving.

When to water:

Moderate, even moisture during active growth

Fertilizer:

Feed lightly during active growth and reduce or pause during slow winter growth.

Pruning:

Remove dead, damaged, or crowded growth with clean pruners.

Repotting/rootbound:

Repot when roots circle densely, the plant dries too fast, or growth stalls despite good care.

Best use

Beneficial insect support and small-space bioactive cover

Zones: USDA Zones 5-10

Season: warm-season

Planting window: Sow after frost when soil is warm, usually late spring through midsummer

Termination: Chop or mow before heavy seed set and use as surface mulch, compost input, or green manure

Nitrogen: Usually not nitrogen-fixing; used more for quick biomass, flowers, pollinator support, or specialty soil roles

Uses: Beneficial insect support and small-space bioactive cover. Use as mulch, compost input, soil biology support, erosion control, or rotation support depending on the crop and the timing.

Cover CropsEasy

Zone timing

Use these windows as a starting point and adjust for local frost, heat, and rainfall.

Zones 2-4

Sow

Late May to July after frost and once soil has warmed well

Manage

Grow through the warmest part of summer

Terminate

August to September, or let frost kill it if a clean winterkill mulch is useful

Zones 5-6

Sow

May to early July after frost

Manage

Run through summer for shade and biomass

Terminate

August to October or before the first hard frost

Zones 7-8

Sow

April to July

Manage

Use in main summer windows or between spring and fall crops

Terminate

July to October before reseeding or before cool-season planting

Zones 9-11

Sow

March to August depending on rainfall and heat

Manage

Use as a hot-weather smother or soil-building crop

Terminate

Before seed set, before the next crop, or before the cool-season transition

Problems to watch

Watch for aphids, mites, scale, mealybugs, fungus gnats, leaf spot, and root rot.
