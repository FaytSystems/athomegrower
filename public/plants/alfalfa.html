Alfalfa

Medicago sativa

Alfalfa is a multi-season deep-rooted perennial legume cover crop added to Fill My Space, Plant Facts, Zones, and Plant Care.

Plant care

Light:

Give the plant the light listed in the quick facts and adjust gradually if leaves fade, stretch, or scorch.

Soil mix:

Deep, well-drained, near-neutral soil

Soil moisture:

Avoid leaving roots in stale, soggy soil unless the plant is specifically moisture-loving.

When to water:

Moderate moisture, especially while establishing

Fertilizer:

Feed lightly during active growth and reduce or pause during slow winter growth.

Pruning:

Remove dead, damaged, or crowded growth with clean pruners.

Repotting/rootbound:

Repot when roots circle densely, the plant dries too fast, or growth stalls despite good care.

Best use

Long-term soil building and deep nutrient mining

Zones: USDA Zones 3-9

Season: multi-season

Planting window: Spring or late summer

Termination: Repeated mowing, tillage, or occultation; established stands can be stubborn

Nitrogen: Good nitrogen fixer over time

Uses: Forage, nitrogen, deep rooting, pollinator support, compost material

Cover CropsModerate

Zone timing

Use these windows as a starting point and adjust for local frost, heat, and rainfall.

Zones 2-4

Sow

Late spring or late summer depending on the species and your rotation

Manage

Maintain with mowing or selective cutting

Terminate

Whenever the next crop window requires a reset

Zones 5-6

Sow

Spring or early fall

Manage

Can persist as a living cover with mowing

Terminate

When a clean bed or rotation shift is needed

Zones 7-8

Sow

Fall through spring depending on heat

Manage

Use as a longer-lived soil cover in the appropriate season

Terminate

Before it becomes too tough, seedy, or competitive

Zones 9-11

Sow

Cool season for temperate perennials, warm season for tropical perennials

Manage

Keep trimmed to fit the main crop or orchard floor plan

Terminate

Reset before the next major planting or if it becomes overly competitive

Problems to watch

Watch for aphids, mites, scale, mealybugs, fungus gnats, leaf spot, and root rot.

Primarily an outdoor garden, raised bed, field, orchard floor, or landscape soil-building plant.
