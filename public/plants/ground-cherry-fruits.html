Plant care

Light:

Full sun is best for flowering, fruiting, and disease prevention.

Soil mix:

Plant in deep, well-drained soil with organic matter. avoid wet, compacted sites.

Soil moisture:

Even moisture during establishment and fruit sizing.

When to water:

Water deeply during establishment and drought. avoid shallow daily sprinkles.

Fertilizer:

Fertilize lightly based on growth and soil testing. avoid excess nitrogen that encourages weak growth.

Pruning:

Prune in late winter or early spring for structure, light penetration, and removal of dead, damaged, or crossing limbs.

Repotting/rootbound:

For patio trees, use large containers, refresh soil, root-prune when necessary, and avoid letting the root ball dry out.

Best use

Containers and raised beds

Zones: usda zones 4-11

Problems to watch

Fruit trees can face borers, scale, mites, leaf spots, brown rot, scab, cankers, and wildlife pressure depending on species.
